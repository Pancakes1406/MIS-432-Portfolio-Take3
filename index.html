AI in Business
Portfolio

Western Washington University · College of Business and Economics

This portfolio documents seven labs I completed in MIS 432, each built around a real company using AI to solve a real business problem. Across the quarter I built working prototypes, audited AI outputs, designed governance frameworks, and tested systems to the point of failure. What I learned is that the gap between AI hype and AI implementation is almost always a data problem, a process problem, or a human oversight problem — never just a technology problem.

What I Learned

Big-Picture Takeaways

01

Every assumption at the data stage ends up embedded in the decision. In the Uber lab, a missing feature for a new neighborhood cascaded forward silently for weeks — drivers were earning less on routes the algorithm confidently sent them to, and riders were waiting on streets the model didn't know existed yet.

02

The algorithm doesn't evaluate quality — it evaluates data. EveryCure can't find a drug-disease match without molecular relationship data. Airbnb can't surface Elena's listing without booking signals. Spotify can't recommend without behavioral history. Cold start isn't a bug, it's a structural feature of how these systems work.

03

One line in a system prompt is the difference between a trustworthy agent and a liability. When I deactivated the patient chart tool in the agentic AI lab, the agent dropped from Level 3 to Level 2 instantly — not because it got dumber, but because its access changed. And without the no-hallucination guardrail, it would have fabricated clinical evidence that looked completely professional.

04

Optimization without context is just automation with consequences. Uber's surge algorithm called 4.8x during a simulated earthquake because it saw the same supply-demand gap it always sees — it had no category for "emergency." Netflix's A/B system picks the highest-CTR thumbnail with no way to know if it's misleading. The hardest governance question is never "does it work?" It's "does it know when to stop?"
